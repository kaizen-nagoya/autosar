Q1 Are there any parts or function of vehicle other than Seat Belts, Air Bags, Automated Vehicles for Safet, Tires and Driver Assistance Technologies? Please show me more than 16 items. 

A1　by gemini
While the "big names" like airbags and seatbelts get most of the credit, modern vehicle safety is a complex web of structural engineering, lighting, and mechanical backups. 

Here are more than 16 additional vehicle parts and functions that play a critical role in safety:

### **Structural & Exterior Safety**
1.  **Crumple Zones:** Areas at the front and rear of the vehicle designed to deform and absorb energy during an impact, protecting the cabin.
2.  **Safety Cage (Survival Cell):** The reinforced center structure of the car designed to remain intact even if the exterior panels are crushed.
3.  **Active Hoods (Pedestrian Protection):** Systems that slightly lift the hood during a collision with a pedestrian to provide a cushion from the hard engine block below.
4.  **Tempered & Laminated Glass:** Side windows are designed to shatter into dull granules, while windshields are laminated to prevent objects from piercing the cabin.
5.  **Side Impact Beams:** High-strength steel bars inside the doors that protect passengers during a "T-bone" collision.
6.  **Collapsible Steering Column:** A steering wheel shaft designed to compress upon impact so it doesn't get pushed into the driver's chest.

### **Mechanical & Braking Systems**
7.  **Anti-lock Braking System (ABS):** Prevents wheels from locking up during hard braking, allowing the driver to maintain steering control.
8.  **Electronic Brake-force Distribution (EBD):** Automatically varies the amount of force applied to each wheel based on weight distribution and road conditions.
9.  **Brake Assist (BA):** Detects emergency braking by the speed at which the pedal is pressed and applies maximum boost to stop the car faster.
10. **Electronic Stability Control (ESC):** Uses sensors to detect loss of traction and automatically applies brakes to individual wheels to keep the car on its intended path.
11. **Brake Override System:** A software fail-safe that prioritizes the brake signal if both the accelerator and brake pedals are pressed simultaneously.

### **Visibility & Communication**
12. **Adaptive Headlights:** Headlights that swivel in the direction of the steering wheel to illuminate curves and corners.
13. **Daytime Running Lights (DRL):** Low-energy lights that stay on during the day to make the vehicle more visible to other drivers and pedestrians.
14. **Auto-Dimming Mirrors:** Rearview and side mirrors that darken automatically to prevent the driver from being blinded by high beams behind them.
15. **High-Mount Stop Lamp (The Third Brake Light):** Positioned at eye level for following drivers to reduce the reaction time for rear-end collisions.

### **Occupant & Child Safety**
16. **LATCH System:** (Lower Anchors and Tethers for CHildren) A standardized system for securing child safety seats without using the adult seat belts.
17. **Active Head Restraints:** Headrests that move forward and upward during a rear-end collision to reduce the risk of whiplash.
18. **Internal Trunk Release:** A glow-in-the-dark handle inside the trunk that allows someone trapped inside to exit.
19. **Rear Door Child Safety Locks:** Mechanical switches that prevent passengers (usually children) from opening the rear doors from the inside.

### **Monitoring Systems**
20. **Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS):** An electronic system that alerts the driver the moment a tire's pressure drops below a safe level.
21. **Driver Drowsiness Detection:** Systems that monitor steering patterns or eye movement to warn the driver if they are showing signs of fatigue.
