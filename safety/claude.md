**Structural & Passive Safety** — crumple zones, safety cages, side-impact door beams, padded dashboards, laminated windshields, and head restraints all work passively to protect occupants during a crash without requiring any action from the driver.

**Braking & Stability** — ABS, Electronic Stability Control (ESC), traction control, Electronic Brakeforce Distribution (EBD), brake assist, and rollover protection all work together to keep the vehicle controllable in emergency situations.

**Visibility & Lighting** — adaptive headlights, backup cameras, and daytime running lights address the significant number of crashes caused by poor visibility.

**Fire & Fuel Safety** — the inertia fuel cut-off switch, fire suppression systems, and EV battery management systems specifically address fire risk, which is especially relevant for both traditional and electric vehicles.

**Electrical & Alert Systems** — TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring), electronic immobilisers, and eCall emergency systems round out the list with active monitoring and communication features.
